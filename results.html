Case Study 01 · Amazon Australia · PPC

Sponsored Products audit: ACOS 88% → 69%

A home & kitchen product on Amazon AU was burning ad spend. A full audit of Search Term, Targeting and Advertised Product reports revealed three structural problems.

Problem 1

Self-cannibalisation

Auto and manual campaigns were bidding against each other on the same terms, inflating CPC with zero incremental sales.

Problem 2

Zero-order spend

A significant share of budget was going to search terms with high clicks and zero orders over the full data window.

Problem 3

Conversion ceiling

A 1-star review was capping conversion regardless of ad quality — a listing-side issue no bid change could fix.

What was done, and what changed

MetricBeforeAfter RestructureChange
ACOS~88%~69%▼ 19 pts
Conversion Rate (CVR)~4.8%~6.4%▲ +33%
Campaign structureOverlapping auto/manualSegmented, negatives addedFixed
Winning search termsBuried in autoHarvested into exact matchScaled

Actions: restructured bids, added negative keywords against zero-order terms, harvested converting search terms into dedicated exact-match campaigns, and flagged the review problem as the true conversion bottleneck. Audits repeat on a scheduled cycle using fresh reports.

Case Study 02 · OnBuy · Software

OnBuy Profit Calculator — a public, verifiable tool

OnBuy sellers had no reliable way to see true margins while browsing listings. So I built a Chrome extension that calculates real OnBuy fees and profit directly on-page.

The critical discipline: every fee and category rule was verified against live OnBuy Seller Central data before shipping — because a profit calculator that's slightly wrong is worse than no calculator.

It's published on the Chrome Web Store (currently v3.6) — proof of both marketplace knowledge and the ability to ship working software, not just talk about it.
